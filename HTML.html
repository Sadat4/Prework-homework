IBM Personal Computer, 1981

The computer that kickstarted the whole third-party revolution of ‘IBM compatible’ accessories, the IBM Personal Computer was the first computer to be officially named a PC. Despite having its own superior parts and operating system, IBM uncharacteristically used Intel’s 8088 processor, off-the-shelf parts and Microsoft’s DOS, and in doing so left its PC open.

Its overnight success prompted an explosion of compatible software, hardware and peripherals leaving everything else in its wake, spawning the burgeoning PC industry.Photograph: Alamy

The base factory configuration included:

  • CPU
  • RAM
  • Video
  • Display
  • Sound
  • Storage
  • Input
  • Expansion
  • Communication
